The Residenza Aldo Capitini is a modern student collegiate residence located in Florence, Italy, and is intimately tied to the Scuola Normale Superiore, a prestigious academic institution primarily based in Pisa. The residence was officially inaugurated on December 1, 2015. Its creation was the direct result of an important academic expansion when the Italian Institute of Human Sciences merged into the Scuola Normale Superiore in 2014. This merger established the school's Department of Political and Social Sciences, which operates out of Palazzo Strozzi in Florence. To accommodate the influx of undergraduate and doctoral students attending this new branch, the residence was built with co-financing from the Italian Ministry of Education, Universities, and Research. The building is named in honor of Aldo Capitini, a distinguished alumnus of the Scuola Normale Superiore who lived from 1899 to 1968. Capitini was a prominent twentieth-century philosopher, pedagogist, and staunch anti-fascist. Often referred to as the Italian Gandhi, he is best remembered for pioneering the philosophy of non-violence in Italy and organizing the first Perugia-Assisi Peace March in 1961. Naming the residence after him was a deliberate choice by the institution to honor his enduring legacy of peace, civic duty, and intellectual rigor. Located at Viale Corsica 100 in the Rifredi neighborhood of Florence, the residence spans a total surface area of 2,200 square meters. It was specifically designed to replicate the tight-knit collegiate environment that the school is famous for in Pisa. The building features twenty-four mini-apartments, including accommodations specially equipped for students with disabilities, alongside ample communal areas meant to encourage interdisciplinary discussions, studying, and leisure. Today, it continues to serve as the primary collegiate hub for students studying in Florence, maintaining the institution's centuries-old tradition of providing scholars with fully funded room, board, and a vibrant academic community.
